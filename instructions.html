Hi!

This is the instruction page for the tools on this site. If you'd like to return to the homepage, click here. If you'd like to go to one of the tools, click one of the below links!

Vector Tool, Transformation Tool, Physics Tool, Rotation Tool.


Vector Tool

Click the various fields to edit the positions of the vectors.

Click the buttons at the top to change between modes, and click the 'Switch to 3D' button to switch to the 3D mode.

Click the 'Clear Inputs' button to reset all of the inputted values.


Transformation Tool

Click the 'Add Transform' button to add a transformation to the transformation bar. Click the 'Edit" button on the transformation to change its values.

Multiple transformations can be added in a sequence. Press the play button, or scrub the playback bar back and forth to view the sequence.

If you want to see all the transformations happen at the same time, click the 'Simultaneous' checkbox at the bottom left.

Click the 'Loop' checkbox if you want to view your transformation sequence in a loop while playing.

At any point while playback is paused, click and drag on any value on the top of the screen left and right to change the value, and see how it affects the model.


Physics Tool

Right click and drag to rotate the camera.

Simulation Mode:

While in simulation mode, you can set the simulation parameters in the top right, by clicking and dragging on the numbers left or right.

You can change the ball's initial position, rotation, velocity and acceleration, as well as the simulation duration and framerate.

You can also enable/disable the floor, gravity, and 'Fun Stuff' to play around with (but why would you ever turn those off?!?!)

When you're ready to run your simulation, click the 'Simulate' button, and then click 'Switch to Playback Mode'.

Playback Mode:

While in playback mode, click the 'Play' button the play back your simulation. You can move frame by frame through the simulation with the buttons on either side of the play button. Click the 'Loop' checkbox if you want your simulation to loop.

In the top right of the simulation window, you can see the ball's position and velocity. If you want to view your simulation in slow motion, click and drag the 'Time Scale' value above the play/pause button. To reset the time scale to 1, click the 'Reset' button.

If you want your simulation to be smoothed between frames (especially useful if your simulation was done at a low framerate) tick the 'Smoothing' box.

If you want the camera to follow the ball, tick the 'Camera Follow' box.

If you wish to return to the simulation screen, click the 'Edit Simulation Parameters' button in the lower left.


Rotation Tool

Drag the values under 'Rotation Euler' to rotate the dog. To reset the rotations, click the 'Reset To Identity' button.

Representations of the current rotation in both quaternion and axis-angle format can be seen on the upper right corner of the screen.
